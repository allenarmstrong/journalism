Use the Inverted Pyramid

Once you’ve gathered your research and interview notes, it’s time to arrange everything in the inverted pyramid. This is a great way to outline your news story so it follows a strong journalistic style.

Use this template to collect your notes in order. You should begin with the most important information and work your way down to lesser topics as you go.

Writing the First Draft

Using your outline in the inverted pyramid, you can get started writing. At this point, you should determine whether you are using a formal or an informal style.

Formal Writing
Formal writing is what you see in most standard news stories. It expresses information about topics that may be considered important to the community. Think of The New York Times, CNN, and NPR. Most parts of these news organizations will use formal writing.
  • Formal writing does NOT use contractions.
  • Formal writing does NOT use slang.
  • Formal writing does NOT sound conversational.
Informal Writing
Informal writing is much more conversational. This often expresses information about “gossip” topics, such as celebrity news, or some sports stories. Think of TMZ, ESPN, and Cracked Magazine.
  • Informal writing doesn’t have a problem with contractions.
  • Informal writing’s cool with slang.
  • And, yeah, informal writing can be conversational.
You will have to decide which type of news story you’re writing. However, there are some guidelines that all good writing should follow:
  • Both formal and informal news writing use complete sentences.
  • Both formal and informal news writing use paragraphs.
  • Both formal and informal news writing use proper grammar, spelling, and punctuation.
Most importantly, remember the following tip: All news writing should be clear for the reader to understand.
