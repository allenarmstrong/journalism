Interviewing is a tricky—but necessary—process that requires preparation and practice. To conduct a good interview, you need to do four things:

When someone you interview expresses opinion, it’s a good idea to find another interviewee who can offer the opposing point of view. This will lend credibility to your story. Whenever possible, gather information from several different kinds of people. If one person is in favor of an initiative, find someone else who isn’t and interview them both.

Note: Remember that you are reporting information to people who may know very little about it. While you will use your research to become well versed in the details of your topic, you may still need to explain the basic facts to your audience.
