Making Revisions

Um…why should I have to revise? It’s a first draft!

Good question, but first drafts are more than your error-ridden first attempt at writing. We all start off with something pretty rough when we first start writing, but before we can call it a polished first draft, we have to check for errors.

Always remember: if you haven’t checked it for basic errors, it’s not ready for your teacher/editor to see it!

Here are some important things to check before you submit your first draft.

AP Stylebook

The Associated Press Stylebook provides the main source of accepted writing guidelines for journalists. Many reporters keep this book at their desks, and copyeditors use it to check for certain rules, such as hyphens and capitalizations. Take a look at the Purdue Online Writing Lab for some important style rules you’ll need to follow.
