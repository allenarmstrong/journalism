Reporter's Notebook Part Two

25 points

  Mastery Achieved Approaching Mastery Needs Improvement
Demonstration of Understanding 5–4 points

The student has written a 20- to 30-word lead sentence for an original news story about a career in journalism. The response shows a basic understanding of proper lead format.		 1–0 points
The student has not written a lead sentence for an original news story about a career in journalism. The student should seek guidance from his or her instructor on this assignment.
5Ws and One H 5–4 points
The student’s lead thoroughly and clearly answers the following questions: Who, What, When, Where, Why, and How.		 3–2 points
The student’s lead addresses the following questions: Who, What, When, Where, Why, and How.		 1–0 points
The student has not responded to the 5Ws and one H. The student should seek guidance from his or her instructor on this assignment.
Impartiality 5–4 points
The student has chosen wording carefully in order to avoid expressing any opinion.		 3–2 points
The student has mostly avoided expressing opinion, though some hints of bias may be evident. 		1–0 points
The student’s writing clearly expresses opinion. The student should seek guidance from his or her instructor on this assignment.
Clarity of Submission 5–4 points
The assignment is easy for the instructor to grade/view. 		3–2 points
The assignment is mostly easy for the instructor to grade/view, with several errors. 		1–0 points
The assignment is difficult or impossible for the instructor to grade/view. The student should seek guidance from his or her instructor on this assignment.
Grammar and Spelling 5–4 points
The student has respected language conventions where necessary. 		3–2 points
The student has respected some language conventions where necessary.		 1–0 points
The student has not respected language conventions where necessary. The student should seek guidance from his or her instructor on this assignment.
