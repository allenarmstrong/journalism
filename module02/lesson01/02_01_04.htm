Getting an Education

In your search for job openings in journalism, you’ll probably find that many positions require some level of higher education and/or experience. Many companies today want new journalists to hold bachelor's degrees in journalism or communications. Some journalists even get graduate degrees to increase their hireability. Internships and other newsroom experience can also be quite helpful to add to your résumé.

Here’s an example of a job posting that has been “unpacked” so that you can see what each part means. Roll over the different elements to view explanations of the listing.

Online Newspaper Associate Editor

Works closely with writers, art directors, and senior editors.

The associate editor must be a journalism professional with excellent communication, organization, editing, and writing skills. Must be able to work both independently and as part of a team. Ability to manage projects,
direct an editorial team, develop new strategies, edit online material, and keep track of website analytics is a must.

A bachelor’s degree in journalism or related field preferred, or a minimum of four years editorial experience in the online news industry. Experience with page layout and online blogging is preferable.

Send a résumé, cover letter, reference letter, and any writing clips to the address below.

Full time with benefits. Equal opportunity employer. Drug-free workplace.

Although interest inventories are helpful in sorting through your preferences, only you can tell what you would most like to do. Also, because every news organization is unique, every position in journalism is a little different. Some places may require a lot of work from certain employees, while other places will require less from the same position. The best way to learn what’s right for you is to research potential careers carefully. Put together a list of things that interest you and compare them with job listings. Soon you’ll have a collection of information about your own interests that can point you in the right direction for your future.
