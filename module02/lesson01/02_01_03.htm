Expert's Corner

Manning Pynn started in journalism as a copy boy.

"Today they call them clerks," he said. One of his duties was to fill paste pots. "We used to type things on something called a typewriter, and then we would glue the pages together with paste, and you had to have these little paste pots on every desk."

He moved up from there to serve at several major newspapers in many different capacities. From his job as a copy boy, Pynn made a lifelong career.
