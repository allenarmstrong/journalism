Your Career Path

After studying journalism, you may find that it interests you as a possible career choice. Great! It’s important to think ahead about your career path. There are many different things to consider:

What job interests you?

Conduct some research into the different positions that are available in journalism. Keep in mind that some skill sets remain the same for long periods of time, but others change as consumers of news change. For example, many newspaper positions now require some understanding of online communications, in addition to traditional media.

Which medium do you prefer?

Think about your interests and your natural abilities. Which news medium is the most interesting to you? Does radio seem like the most exciting position, or would you rather work at a newspaper or television station? Are you interested in something that is strictly online? You should also research what experts suggest the future may hold for each medium. Is one medium gaining a larger audience, while others are shrinking?

What jobs are out there?

You can check for job openings in a number of ways. Many websites specialize in finding and listing job openings from around the world. You can enter search options for what kind of position you’re seeking. Another way is to contact the organizations that interest you most and ask whether they are accepting applications for new journalists. Be sure to get as much information as possible so you’re prepared when you apply. Finally, consider taking on an internship in order to gain required experience.

What associations can help me?

Research respected associations for journalists and review their information. Many places such as the
Poynter Institute offer articles about changing trends in the industry, information about how to improve your skills, and names of contacts in journalism who might have more useful information.

What salary will I earn?

Remember that anyone starting out in any industry is likely to make a small salary. Normally, as your experience grows, so will your income. You may want to take a look at the PayScale Salary Calculator to see what the average pay is for someone with the title you want in the city where you live. Compare that pay with other jobs. What are my skills?

If you know you’re an excellent writer, you may want to consider reporting. If your talents are more visual, you may want to consider photography or page layout. Take a close look at what you know you can do well, and ask yourself what skills you want to develop. This may help you decide what you’d be happiest doing for a living.
