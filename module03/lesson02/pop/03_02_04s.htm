Semicolons

Let’s say you have a comma splice that you don’t want to turn into two different sentences. Instead, you would like to keep the “flow” of the thoughts together. There is a way to do this without breaking any rules!

The semicolon does what the comma cannot in these situations. Think of the semicolon as being a little bit stronger than the comma; it is able to join two related thoughts together in a sentence.

There are two important rules to follow when you are using semicolons:

  • Both ideas must be independent clauses. You cannot put a dependent clause on one side of a semicolon.
  • Both ideas must be related. You cannot write, "I like bicycles; where’s my cheese?"
