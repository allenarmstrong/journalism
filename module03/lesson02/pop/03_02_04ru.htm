Run-on Sentences

Run-on sentences are also grammatically incorrect.

A run-on sentence is a sentence that includes more than one idea without joining them together properly. It combines two or more independent clauses without using appropriate connections.

Run-on Sentences

Run-on: I went to the store I was hungry.
Correct: I went to the store because I was hungry.

Run-on: I'd been walking for hours I was exhausted.
Correct: Since I'd been walking for hours, I was exhausted.

There are several ways to resolve run-on sentences:

  • Break them into separate sentences.
  • Add in joining words (and, or, but, yet).
  • Use a semicolon.

Take a look at this example and the resolutions: My good friend loves playing basketball, beach volleyball, and soccer he recently hurt himself and cannot play for months.

Run-on Resolutions

  • Separate sentences: My good friend loves playing basketball, beach volleyball, and soccer. He recently hurt himself and cannot play for months.
  • Add joining words: My good friend loves playing basketball, beach volleyball, and soccer, but he recently hurt himself and cannot play for months.
  • Use a semicolon: My good friend loves playing basketball, beach volleyball, and soccer; he recently hurt himself and cannot play for months.
