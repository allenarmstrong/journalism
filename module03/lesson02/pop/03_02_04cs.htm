Comma Splices

There are actually several ways to resolve run-on sentences. You may break them into separate sentences, add in joining words, or use appropriate punctuation.

One common problem that people run into when trying to fix run-on sentences is called a comma splice. This is when the writer joins two thoughts with just a comma. This is not a correct way to fix a sentence. Comma splices are also errors in sentence structure.

Take a look at the following examples of comma splices:

  • I went to the store, I was hungry.
  • I'd been walking for hours, I was exhausted.
  • It's time to go to the movies, I can't wait.
  • Let's talk to Marcia, she knows what's going on.
