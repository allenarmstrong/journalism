Final Draft

Congratulations! You have planned, researched, and written a first draft of your feature story. That’s an amazing accomplishment!

Now it’s time to revise your work. Your final draft should show major revision changes from your first draft, and it will be a much better product.

First Draft

Your first draft was the structured collection of your ideas for a feature story. There were probably some errors, but you corrected many of them. In your first draft, your thoughts may not have flowed together as smoothly as you would have liked, but you included your research, interviews, descriptive details, and anecdotes to get your message across.

Final Draft

Think of your final draft as the publishable revision of your first draft. There should be no errors, and your thoughts should flow together as smoothly as possible. You will use effective transitions and word choices to show your reader what is happening.

Even though you’ve made revisions throughout the writing process, this is the stage where you should make the strongest choices to revise and improve your work.

The Finishing Touches

Your next steps will be to revise and edit your work. As you can see below, revision is a much more involved process than editing.

Revising requires you to look at your writing in a different way to see what can be improved.

Editing is the final step in the process, where you go back to correct any conventions errors: spelling, grammar, capitalization, and punctuation.

Both steps are extremely important to the strength of your final draft!

Remember to use Active Sentence Structure

The subject of the sentence is active. It is performing the action.
