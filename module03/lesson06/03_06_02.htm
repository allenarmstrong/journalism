Who’s a Public Speaker?

You are!

There are many situations where people are considered public speakers. Check out who should be prepared.

Practice Makes Perfect

One of the most important things you can do to prepare for this public speaking assignment is to rehearse your presentation before you record it.

Try reading your presentation straight through out loud, or breaking it up with a slideshow or other visual aids that illustrate your major points. However you plan to communicate your feature story to your audience, make sure you practice several times before you create the real thing. This can help you get any nervous feelings out, and it will allow you to identify areas for improvement.

Your Image

If you’re nervous, confused, or rushing through the presentation, your audience will know.

The way you sound and appear will convey either confidence or lack of confidence. Practice using a calm tone of voice, and plan to wear something that makes you look professional if you will be seen. Remember to avoid common pauses such as "um" and "uh." Try to project an image of complete control over your presentation.
