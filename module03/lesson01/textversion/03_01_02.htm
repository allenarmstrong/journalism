01.01 Introduction to the Course - Text Version

Male interviewee #1: What do I think journalism is?

Female interviewee #1: Journalism is reporting news and current events either through photography, print, radio, television, or other forms of media.

Male interviewee #1: Is it about taking a long “journey-lism”?

Female interviewee #2: It’s how you look at people; how you judge them.

Male interviewee #2: Journalism today is a business.

Female interviewee #2: That’s journalism.

Female interviewee #3: …And it can be in the form of newspapers, the Internet…

Male interviewee #3: Journalism is the recording of history, hopefully objective, without opinion.

Female interviewee #2: Journalism can be true, to the eye, and it can be false; it’s how you look at it.

Female interviewee #3: I think journalism is the study of how news media collects information.

Female interviewee #4: Journalism to me is a recording.

Male interviewee #2: You’re journaling events. You’re cataloging events in time: people, places, things.

Female interviewee #4: When I do my own journal, I’m recording my own life, and I expect others to learn and grow and be provoked.

Female interviewee #5: I think that journalism is your opportunity to share the truth.
