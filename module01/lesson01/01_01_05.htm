Symbolic Expression


Freedom of speech is often a contentious issue, and Americans frequently debate the details of the First Amendment. For example, “symbolic expression” is also protected by the First Amendment. This means that forms of expression that do not include speech, such as demonstrations, may also fall under the protection of the freedom of speech.

In addition, courts have had to establish buffer zones in some situations, requiring protesters to stay a certain distance from the event they are protesting. This and other restrictions are established to diminish the chance of violence during protests.

Sometimes freedom of speech is confusing. Are there some instances in which freedom of speech should not be allowed? Take a look at the following scenarios to try to determine what you would do if the decision were up to you.

Things to Consider

  • Because pleasant speech is rarely contested, the First Amendment is designed to protect speech that may seem offensive to some.
  • Any right you deny someone is a right that someone else may take from you later.
  • Ask yourself what the downside is of not being able to criticize or question people in authority.

Examples of Tricky First Amendment Issues

  • Someone is burning an American flag in the street. Should that person be arrested, or is this act an example of freedom of speech?
  • Residents of your community have rallied to have a book removed from the school’s library shelves. The book uses several racial slurs and depicts violent actions against children. Should the book be banned, or is it protected by freedom of speech?
  • A popular celebrity appears on a famous children’s show, but she wears clothing that some may consider revealing for her performance. Should the episode be canceled, or is her outfit an example of freedom of speech?
  • A new, first-person shooter video game includes several scenes of graphic violence. A pacifist group feels that the game glorifies killing and asks that the developers tone down the gore. Should the developers reduce the violence, or is this protected by freedom of speech?
  • A whistleblower brings a news reporter a classified document that proves wrongdoing at the highest level of government. The bureau involved asks the reporter not to air the story. Should the news story be canceled, or is this an example of freedom of speech?

As you can see, freedom of speech and freedom of the press can sometimes be challenging to define. Many people face this issue in their daily lives. Journalists need to be aware of their rights and the restrictions surrounding them in order to ethically and responsibly report the news.
