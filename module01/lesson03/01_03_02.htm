Changes

The 1920s saw the advent of radio broadcast journalism and, a few years later, television broadcast journalism. Much as the Internet has done today, early advancements in technology changed the face of journalism forever.

Photojournalism

Wartime has always been important for journalism. People want to know how their loved ones in the service are doing, and they want to know why their leaders are sending them to fight. The Civil War marked an important change in the history of American journalism. Not only did reporters follow the battles and write about what had happened on the front lines for the first time, but photographers contributed images that became some of the first examples of photojournalism in America.

Today, photojournalism continues to be a major source of our news. Though pictures themselves do not convey all the information needed, they help readers understand what is going on in their communities and throughout the world.

Radio

Radio broadcast news provides a voice to accompany the stories, but no pictures. This means that reporters on the radio have to enunciate and describe events in vivid detail. In the early days of radio reporting, people often gathered around their radios to listen to the evening news each day. Today, the radio still reports about current events, weather, traffic updates, and other topics.

Television

Television provides voice and video to cover the news, but not written stories. This means that reporters on television must look professional and describe events clearly alongside video clips of news as it happens. Television still provides the news in many forms today.

Internet News

Finally, with the ever rising popularity of the Internet, news has gone mobile. Now people can choose where and when they get their news from many different types of media, including print, radio, television, websites, blogs, and more.

On the Internet, online versions of most traditional formats are available. People can read newspapers online. Radio channels broadcast over the Internet. Television provides streaming clips and sometimes entire episodes. In addition to these options, people can receive news from news websites, blogs, and text message updates.
