Gatekeepers

Matthew Brady (1822 – 1896)

Type of Journalist: Photojournalist
Know for:  Brady’s photographs of depictions of death and destruction increased the public’ interest in news about the Civil War.
Quote: “No one will ever know what I went through to secure those negatives. The world can never appreciate it.  IT changes the whole course of my life.” – Matthew Brady

Edward R. Murrow (1908 – 1965)

Type of Journalist: Radio Broadcast Journalist
Know for:  Murrow’s sense of ethics, clear speaking voice, and strong reporting skills earned him the respect of audiences and fellow journalists in both radio and TV.
Quote: “Good night, and good luck.”  - Edward R. Murrow

Bob Woodward (Born 1943) and Carl Bernstein (Born 1944)

Type of Journalist: Investigative Reporters
Know for: Washington Post reporter Woodward and Bernstein’s extensive research led to one of the largest political scandals in the nation’s history: Watergate. As a result of their strong investigative reporting, Richard Nixon resigned his position as president.
Quote: “The central dilemma in journalism is that you don’t know what you don’t know.” – Bob Woodward

Anderson Cooper (Born 1967)

Type of Journalist: Television News Anchor
Know for:  Cooper’s coverage of Hurricane Katrina set him apart from many other reporters when he expressed emotion and anger on screen about the people suffering in New Orleans.
Trivia: Before earning fame as a journalist, Cooper was the host of a reality TV show called The Mole.
