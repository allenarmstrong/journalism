Fortunes in Journalism

In the late 1800s and early 1900s, people had developed such an interest in news that it became possible to make a huge fortune as a publisher or editor in the journalism industry. One notable person to do this was William Randolph Hearst.

William Randolph Hearst made his fortune through sensationalizing journalism. He took over several publications and showed more of an interest in sales than in credibility. Many of his newspapers exploited stories to make them seem more sensational than they were in reality. This practice led to what became known as yellow journalism.

By the 1950s, many homes in the United States had at least one television, and the demand for regular news updates grew. In the 1980s, media tycoon Ted Turner invested in Cable News Network, or CNN. This became the first channel to provide 24-hour news coverage.

Billionaire Rupert Murdoch became a household name as he built his media empire through the acquisition of several companies. As CEO of News Corporation, Murdoch came to own Fox News, the New York Post newspaper, HarperCollins Publishers, the 20th Century Fox film and TV studios, MySpace, and many other media sources.
