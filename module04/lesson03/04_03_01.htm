The Lesson





Every journalist has to develop good speaking and listening skills. It’s part of the job. Especially when covering someone else’s speech, a journalist has to be able to understand what’s being said in order to find out what seems true and what doesn’t. This close listening skill will help when it’s time to check the facts!

For the assignment, you will evaluate a speech. Take a look at the specific assignment instructions below.

Objectives

After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

  • differentiate between logos, pathos, and ethos
  • identify claim, counterclaim, and rebuttal in an argument
  • evaluate a speech for effectiveness
