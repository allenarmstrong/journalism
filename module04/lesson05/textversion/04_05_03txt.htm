04.05 The Rebuttal–Text Version

When you write the rebuttal to your opinion piece, it's really important to first consider your claim and counterclaim.

Think of it this way—you wouldn't tell a story without a main character, or take a picture without a subject, right?

Your claim is the foundation of your argument—it's the main point. Without it, your opinion is lost!

Your counterclaim is equally important. This is where you bring up the opposition to your claim. A good argument always shows both sides and lets the reader decide which one to believe.

Your rebuttal will respond to the counterclaim. This is where you will disagree with the opposing side. To write a clear rebuttal, you must negate the counterclaim and say WHY.

A strong rebuttal should leave your audience with a clear understanding of your position. To give it credibility, include evidence such as researched facts, specific details, historical information, or quotes from reliable sources.

Remember, the details of your rebuttal will depend on your stance, or opinion.

If you are challenging someone else's opinion, provide evidence to back it up.

If you are challenging assumptions, explain why they don't hold up.

So when you write your rebuttal to prove your point, make sure you can support it.
