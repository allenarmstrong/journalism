Quotations

Including a quotation from a source can be useful when you want to express an idea with credibility. If an expert can sum up an important fact, you include a quotation to show your audience that you’ve done your research.

There’s a specific way to format quotations in your writing.

First, make sure your audience knows who is talking. You can do this with signal words and phrases.

Second, include the writer’s or speaker’s exact words in quotation marks. If the quotation comes at the end of the sentence, the period (or other ending punctuation) should appear inside the quotation marks.

Finally, follow up on the quote to help your audience understand what it means or why you included it.
